I cancelled Cursor and redirected that $20/month into Ollama Cloud—hosting a roster of models I can switch between instantly.

Instead of paying for a single “premium” workflow, I’m now running a **Streamlit app** that gives me access to **36 models** on Ollama Cloud, including:

- Kimi 2.6  
- DeepSeek V4  
- Qwen 3.6  
- and other high-performing Chinese models that get endlessly dunked on… while quietly proving they can match (and sometimes beat) US corporate “frontier” models for real work.

## What changed for me

The biggest difference is **optionality**.

When I’m coding, writing, or doing research, I don’t want one model—I want the *right* model for the moment:

- fast + cheap for iteration
- strong reasoning when I’m stuck
- good writing when I’m polishing
- good coding assistance when I’m shipping

## The Streamlit “model switchboard”

I built a small Streamlit “switchboard” so I can 

- pick a model from a dropdown
- keep prompts consistent
- compare outputs quickly
- route tasks to the model that fits best

[github repo]((https://github.com/lislema/ollama-chat-client) )

This has been saving me a **huge amount of money**, and it’s also more fun—because I can actually *play* with the ecosystem instead of being locked into one vendor.

## Claude Code, but at discount rates

One surprise upside: I can still run **Claude Code** when I want, but I’m using it more strategically—so the overall spend stays low.

## Next: skills and automation

Now that the cost pressure is gone, I’ve got time to experiment with:

- skills / tool-use patterns
- lightweight agents
- small automations that actually fit my workflow

More to come.
